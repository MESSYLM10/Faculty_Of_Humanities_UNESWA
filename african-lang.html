African Languages and Literature

Preserving and promoting African linguistic heritage while advancing literary scholarship and cultural studies.

About the Department

The Department of African Languages and Literature stands as a guardian of Africa's rich linguistic and literary heritage. We are committed to preserving, studying, and promoting the indigenous languages and literatures of Africa, with special emphasis on Eswatini's cultural traditions.

Our department plays a crucial role in maintaining cultural identity while preparing students for careers in education, translation, cultural preservation, and literary studies. We bridge the gap between traditional knowledge systems and contemporary academic scholarship.

Mission

To preserve, study, and promote African languages and literatures while fostering cultural pride and linguistic competence for academic and professional success.

Vision

To be the premier center for African language and literature studies in Southern Africa, recognized for excellence in cultural preservation and scholarly achievement.
